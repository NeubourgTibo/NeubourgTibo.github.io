The nomination

April 5, 2022

After many mails, conversations and a Go Abroad Fair, the time had come. The The main part of my programme had been made and partly approved. Enough to nominate me , so I can enrol at TU Dublin. Putting together the programme was a long long process that is still going on. The information you can find is extremely limited, so that an email had to be sent to every part of the programme. Fortunately, I usually received a quick response. There was also a close follow-up by the anchor person who asked for a state of affairs at various times and helped where necessary.


Another step closer!

Green light!

February 8, 2022

The time has come, today I received the green light from my studies. I passed all my exams and had an average of 74%, enough to officially start the Erasmus adventure. After an information session, it became clear that the country itself has not yet been decided. A lot can still happen and I can still choose from the other countries.


The first step has been taken!

Citytrip Dublin

last-minute trip January 31, February 1-2, 2022

Dublin had been on my bucket list for a long time, and during the lesson-free week I finally made it and went to Dublin last minute. On the agenda: Guiness Storehouse, various walking routes and good food. After walking 46 kilometres in three days, we had seen almost all the sights (St Patrick's Cathedral, HA'PENNY BRIDGE, TRINITY COLLEGE, Christ Church Cathedral, Temple Bar, Dublin Castle,...).

The request

Dublin, or Malta? December 14, 2021

March 14, 1 day before the deadline. After a long search and doubts, the decision was made. From the list of countries, I have chosen Ireland and Malta. Each of them has its own charms and attractions. Dublin has been a bucket list item for some time, but Malta has also been on the list of places to visit.
After writing my motivation letter and translating my CV, my application could could be done. Now I had to wait for the exam results and the advice I would get.
